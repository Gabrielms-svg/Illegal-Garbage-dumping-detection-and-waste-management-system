Waste Awareness

Types of Waste & Safe Disposal Methods

1. Biodegradable Waste

Includes food scraps, garden waste, and other organic matter. Dispose of it through composting or bio-degradable bins to reduce landfill impact.

2. Recyclable Waste

Paper, plastic, glass, and metal should be cleaned and sent to recycling centers. Avoid mixing with food waste for effective recycling.

3. Hazardous Waste

Includes batteries, paints, chemicals, and e-waste. Dispose only at authorized collection points to prevent environmental contamination.

4. Electronic Waste (E-Waste)

Old gadgets and appliances contain valuable materials. Drop them off at e-waste recycling centers for safe and eco-friendly disposal.

5. Construction Waste

Includes debris, bricks, and cement. Reuse wherever possible or deliver to designated construction recycling facilities.

6. Biomedical Waste

Generated from hospitals and homes (e.g., syringes, masks). Must be disposed of in color-coded medical waste bins under authorized supervision.
